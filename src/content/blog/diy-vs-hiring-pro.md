<p>
    Borrowing or renting tools adds hassle and cost. If you need a specific
    tool for a one-time project, hiring a pro who already owns it often
    makes more sense.
</p>

<h3>2. What's Your Skill Levelâ€”Honestly?</h3>
<p>

<h3>3. What's the Consequence of Failure?</h3>
<p>
    Painting a door wrong? You can repaint. Wiring an outlet wrong? Your
    house could burn down.
</p>

<h3>4. What's Your Time Worth?</h3>
<p>
    That "2-hour" project often takes 6 hours when you're learning.
    Calculate the real cost of your time.
</p>

<h2>Call a Pro (Worth Every Dollar) ðŸ“ž</h2>
<p>These projects are false economy to DIY:</p>

<h3>Electrical Work</h3>
<p><strong>Always hire a pro.</strong> Electrical mistakes can:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Cause fires</li>
    <li>Electrocute you or your family</li>
    <li>Void insurance if done wrong</li>
    <li>Require expensive fixes</li>
</ul>
<p>
    <strong>Examples:</strong> New outlets, rewiring, panel work, ceiling fans
</p>

<h3>Plumbing (Major)</h3>
<p>Small fixes? Sure. Major work? Call a pro:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Water heater installation</li>
    <li>Main line work</li>
    <li>Gas lines (NEVER DIY)</li>
    <li>Sewer repairs</li>
</ul>
<p>
    <strong>DIY OK:</strong> Replacing faucets, installing bidet, fixing running
    toilets
</p>

<h3>Roofing</h3>
<p>Besides being dangerous, mistakes cost thousands:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Safety risk = serious</li>
    <li>Warranty voids if DIY</li>
    <li>Leaks cause massive damage</li>
    <li>Insurance may not cover DIY damage</li>
</ul>

<h3>HVAC</h3>
<p>
    You can change filters. That's it. Everything else needs certification.
</p>

<h3>Structural Changes</h3>
<p>
    Moving walls, beams, or load-bearing elements requires engineering and
    permits. Not negotiable.
</p>

<p>These could go either way based on your experience:</p>

<ul>
    <li>

    <li>

<h3>Large Drywall Repairs</h3>
<ul>
    <li>

    <li>

    </li>
    <li>

</ul>

<h3>Deck Building</h3>
<ul>

<h3>Kitchen/Bath Remodels</h3>

<h2>Charlotte-Specific Considerations</h2>

<h3>Permits Required</h3>
<p>Charlotte requires permits for:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Electrical work beyond basic fixtures</li>
    <li>Plumbing changes</li>
    <li>Structural modifications</li>
    <li>HVAC installations</li>
    <li>Fences over 6 feet (check local ordinances)</li>
</ul>
<p>
    <strong>Note:</strong> Permitted work usually requires licensed professionals.
</p>

<h3>Insurance Considerations</h3>
<p>
    Some Charlotte insurance companies won't cover damage from unpermitted
    DIY work. Check your policy!
</p>
